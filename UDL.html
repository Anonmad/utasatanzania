United Developmental League (UDL)

About UDL

Ligi ya Maendeleo A community-focused youth football league with one overall winner system, featuring categories U10, U13, U15, and U17. The league is played in Kigoma main and across our village hubs, offering consistent competition and a clear pathway for youth development.

Quick Facts (at-a-glance)

• Age groups: U10, U13, U15, U17
• League system: One division with an overall winner at the end of the season
• Season length: 8–12 weeks
• Matchdays: Weekend fixtures (Saturday &/or Sunday)
• Locations: Kigoma main & Utasatanzania hubs (Rugufu, Rusesa, Kwaga, Bugaga

Objectives: Player development, coach capacity building, community inclusion, safeguarding

About the League

The United Developmental League (UDL) is UTASATANZANIA’s flagship grassroots competition designed with a one-winner system. Players compete in their categories (U10, U13, U15, U17), and the winners from each hub converge in Kigoma main for the decisive matches that crown the ultimate UDL Champion. This structure builds anticipation, ensures fairness, and creates a professional league feel for young players.

UDL connects village hubs, local coaches, and scouts with a clear pathway into the MADT player development programmes. The league emphasizes fairness, safeguarding, and community participation while identifying talent for higher-level trials and educational opportunities.

What we offer

• Regular competitive matches across U10–U17 categories in both hubs and Kigoma main.
• Talent identification: scouts and coaches observe each match to recommend standout players for MADT trials.
• Coach and referee training clinics to raise local technical standards.
• Safeguarding & medical support on matchdays to ensure player safety and wellbeing.
• Inclusive access initiatives to encourage girls, players with disabilities, and low-income participants.

Competition Format (short)

• Hub-level league matches (round-robin in each hub) home and away.
• Top teams from each hub advance to Kigoma main.
• Kigoma main hosts playoffs and finals for each category (U10, U13, U15, U17).
• One overall champion is crowned per season.

How to Register

• Download and complete the registration form (team & player details required).
• Provide player identification (birth certificate or school ID) and emergency contacts.
• Pay the registration fee or present a sponsorship confirmation if subsidised.
• Submit forms at the Utasatanzania office or use our online registration portal.

Fees & Support

We keep fees affordable to maximise participation. Fee options are flexible and include team packages, per-player rates, and sponsored placements for players in need.

Sponsorship opportunities:

Matchday sponsor, Team sponsor, Equipment sponsor, Health & Safety sponsor.

Safeguarding & Health

UDL is committed to a child-safe environment. Our league enforces a safeguarding policy, requires signed guardian consent for all players, and deploys trained first-aiders and a Safeguarding Officer at all venues.

Key policies:

Code of Conduct for players, coaches and spectators; Immediate incident reporting; Medical referral protocol.

Talent Pathway

Outstanding performers in UDL are invited to MADT trials and Elite Pathway assessments. Coaches use a simple performance rubric to evaluate technique, decision-making, attitude, and athletic potential. Selected players enter the MADT development pipeline and benefit from coaching, education links, and exposure to agents and tournaments.

Matchday Experience

• Matches held both in Kigoma main and across hubs.
• Community-focused atmosphere with halftime activities for families and talent showcases.
• Local vendors and small-market stalls encouraged to participate.
• Awards every matchday (MVP, Fair Play) and end-of-season tournaments with trophies and certificates.

Media & Highlights

Follow match highlights, player spotlights, and fixture updates on Utasatanzania social channels. Local radio partners and community bulletin boards also broadcast match results and important announcements.

Alignment with SDGs & Our Theme

The UDL is aligned with Tanzania’s national priorities and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Through football, UDL contributes to:

• SDG 3 (Good Health & Well-being): Encouraging physical activity, healthy competition, and access to safe sport.
• SDG 4 (Quality Education): Linking sport with life skills, discipline, and pathways to educational opportunities.
• SDG 5 (Gender Equality): Ensuring both boys and girls can participate equally in UDL.
• SDG 8 (Decent Work & Economic Growth): Creating opportunities for referees, coaches, vendors, and small businesses on matchdays.
• SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities): Bringing inclusive access to sport across rural hubs and Kigoma main.
• SDG 16 (Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions): Promoting teamwork, fairness, safeguarding, and community participation.
• SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals): Working with local authorities, schools, NGOs, and sponsors to sustain the league.
This alignment reflects UTASATANZANIA’s theme: “Creating Equal Opportunities, Changing Lives Through Sports.”
